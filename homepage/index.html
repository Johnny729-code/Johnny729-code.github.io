The most technologically advanced (and also my favorite) air superiority fighter jet, the F-22 Raptor, is nearly 30 years old! The F-22 was created with the goal of replacing the F-15 Eagle, a nearly 50-year-old fighter, and made its first flight in 1997.

You probably know that Sir Isaac Newton was a brilliant physicist, but did you know that at just the age of 22 years old, he invented one of the most advanced and important subjects in mathematics? Isaac Newton not only created calculus, but also created the binomial theorem, refined the scientifc method, introduced regression analysis, and studied light and optics.

Most people don't know how propeller planes work! An engine inside the plane generates rotational power that spins the propeller using a shaft that connects the two components. The blades of the propeller are designed so that as it spins, it pushes air backwards, creating less pressure in front of it and more pressure behind it. Pressure is a kind of force, so more force behind the plane generates thrust, pushing it forward.

Similarly, most people don't know how jets work. Jets generate thrust using jet engines. Jet engines intake air as the plane moves and compresses it, increasing the air's pressure. The compressed air then moves into the combustion chamber, where fuel is mixed with it and ignited to create a hot, pressurized gas that is pushed out the back of the engine using a nozzle. This gas produces powerful thrust that gives jets the ability to fly at supersonic speeds.
